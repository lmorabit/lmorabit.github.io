I am a post-doctoral research assistant at the University of Oxford, and the Christopher and Lee Millard Postdoctoral Fellow at Christ Church for the 2017/2018 academic year. My main research goal is to answer fundamental questions on how super-massive black holes co-evolve with the galaxies in which they reside. I do this by using low frequency radio observations coupled with multi-wavelength data. I aim to provide new insight using high-resolution radio imaging to clearly distinguish two main components in galaxy evolution: star formation, and high-energy processes associated with super-massive black holes that are actively accumulating material. I am part of Prof Matt Jarvis' research group, which focuses on galaxy evolution and cosmology.

Previously, I completed my Ph.D. at the University of Leiden (2012-2016) under the supervsion of Prof. Huub Röttgering. My thesis work focused on studying radio galaxies at low frequencies using the Low Frequency Array (LOFAR), using both spectroscopy and high resolution imaging.

I earned my M.Sc. in Physics at the University of Oklahoma (2009-2012) under the supervision of Prof. Xinyu Dai. My thesis focused on the link between super-massive black holes and their host galaxies, using X-ray observations and simulations.

I did my undergraduate studies at the University of Michigan, earning a double major in Physics and Astronomy. I had a full scholarship from the US Air Force for my studies, and upon graduation in 2005, I served a six year committment as an officer, in the career field of Air Battle Management (2005-2011).

Originally from Michigan, I lived in six different regions of the USA before moving to the Netherlands and then to Oxford. I enjoy reading, cooking, sewing, running, and outdoor activities.
